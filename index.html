Assumptions (fixed)
  • Property & Tax
  • Property tax: CA Prop 13: 1.25% of purchase price, +2%/yr max
  • Insurance: 0.5% of current property value/yr
  • Maintenance: 1% of current property value/yr
  • Depreciation (rental only): IRS 27.5-yr schedule; improvement % preset per location (county assessor records). Primary residences are not eligible for depreciation deductions.
  • Tax rate: 44% (35% fed + 9.3% CA) on paper rental gain/loss
  • Capital & Returns
  • Initial capital: $100K for all scenarios
  • Wealth = Current Equity + Cumulative Net Cash Flows — equity is home value minus remaining mortgage balance; cash flows are total rent collected minus all expenses paid out of pocket
  • Reinvested mode: positive cash flows compound at S&P 500 monthly returns; deficit periods accumulate additively (treated as out-of-pocket cost, not leveraged against the market)
  • Note: for highly leveraged scenarios (e.g. 25% down with high mortgage rates), net cash flows are often negative throughout — Reinvested and Additive modes will produce identical results since there is no positive pool to compound
  • Additive mode: dividends & rent cash flows accumulate without compounding
  • Refinance
  • Refi count (0–3): number of refinances during the holding period
  • Purchase rate is the 30-yr fixed average for the start year and is locked until a refi occurs
  • Rate rule: selects the N cheapest years to refi from historical data in the chosen period (N = refi count, excl. purchase year); a refi fires only if the new rate is ≥1% below the current rate and at least 2 years have passed since the last refi or purchase
  • Balance (rate-and-term): refi to same remaining balance at the new lower rate
  • LTV (cash-out): new loan = max(remaining balance, 75% of current property value). Difference credited to investor cash position
  • Each refi resets to a new 30-yr amortization at the new rate; no closing costs modeled
  • Source: FRED MORTGAGE30US annual averages
  • Rental + refi (tax interaction): rental mortgage interest is tax-deductible. When interest + depreciation exceed rent income, you have a “paper loss” — you owe no tax and may receive a refund, even though you are depositing rent checks. Refinancing to a lower rate reduces that interest deduction, shrinking the paper loss and its tax refund — partially offsetting the payment savings. In some cases the paper loss flips to taxable profit, adding a tax bill. Net refi benefit for rentals is always lower than the face-value payment savings suggest.
  • Primary + refi: standard deduction assumed — no tax complication. Benefit lands in two places: (1) lower monthly PI reduces cumulative costs, improving the cash-flow component of wealth; (2) however, each refi resets to a new 30-yr amortization, so equity builds more slowly and the loan payoff date extends. Within a typical 30-yr window the equity loss often offsets the payment savings — full net benefit usually requires holding 10+ years past the refi date.

Capital Gains: Long-term rate (federal 20% + NIIT 3.8% + state). Stocks/index: gain = proceeds minus initial investment (cost basis). RE rental: 25% depreciation recapture on prior deductions; LT rate on remaining appreciation. RE Primary: §121 excludes $500K (married) / $250K (single) if owned ≥2 years. 1031 defers all tax on rollover.

    About This Calculator

    This free calculator compares S&P 500, NASDAQ, and 60/40 portfolio returns against real estate investment returns across 54 US locations from 1970 to 2025, with 2026 estimates. It uses FHFA House Price Index and S&P CoreLogic Case-Shiller HPI data alongside historical stock market return data to model five leverage scenarios: all-cash, 35% down, 30% down, 25% down, and 20% down. Rental mode adds NOI calculation, cap rate, IRS 27.5-year depreciation tax shield, property management, HOA, and vacancy. Up to three cash-out refinances can be modeled. Capital gains tax — including the $250K/$500K primary residence exclusion and 1031 exchange deferral — is calculated at sale. The wealth decomposition panel breaks down exactly where each dollar of return came from: price appreciation, leverage amplification, rental income, depreciation tax savings, and dividends.

    Key Findings (1995–2025)

    All-cash California real estate purchases from 1995 to 2025 generally underperformed the S&P 500 total return — averaged ~6–7%/yr appreciation vs S&P 500 ~10–11%/yr total return (1970–2025) — due to low rent yields (3–4%) and no leverage benefit. A 25%-down purchase in Orange County, Irvine, or Los Angeles in 1995 frequently matched or exceeded the S&P 500 on a total-wealth basis — leveraged equity CAGR ~12–15%/yr in coastal CA and WA markets (1995–2025) — driven by 5–7%/yr FHFA appreciation compounded on a 4× leveraged position plus the depreciation tax shield. Bay Area Peninsula markets — Atherton, Palo Alto, Los Altos, Menlo Park — delivered the highest leveraged returns in this dataset, with 7%+/yr FHFA CAGR 1995–2025. Texas markets (Dallas-Fort Worth, Southlake, Frisco) offer substantially higher rent yields (~6%) than California coastal but lower appreciation (~4–5%/yr), and ~2.1%/yr property tax is the largest cost drag. Miami and Florida statewide showed among the strongest recent appreciation — ~10%/yr Case-Shiller CAGR 2020–2025; moderate long-term at ~5%/yr since 1987 — with the Case-Shiller Miami index up over 60% from 2020–2025. Seattle and Bellevue delivered tech-driven appreciation of Case-Shiller Seattle ~6.5%/yr from 1990–2025; no state income tax reduces the depreciation shield value relative to CA or NY. Manhattan and Brooklyn real estate has appreciated at roughly 5–6%/yr since 1987 — highest transaction costs (~5–6%) of any market covered; NY's 50% combined tax rate maximizes the depreciation shield value.

    States Covered

    • California (CA) — Prop 13 property tax (1.25% of purchase price, +2%/yr cap). Combined tax rate 44% (35% fed + 9.3% state). Improvement ratio ~40%. High home price-to-rent ratios make all-cash RE a long-term underperformer vs S&P 500; leveraged scenarios are competitive due to appreciation and depreciation shield.
    • Texas (TX) — Market-rate property tax ~2.1%/yr of assessed value. No state income tax (combined rate 35% fed). Higher rent yields than CA; improvement ratio ~70%. Strong job growth drives home price appreciation.
    • Florida (FL) — Homestead exemption applies to primary residences; rental properties taxed at ~1.1%/yr. No state income tax (35% fed). Coastal demand and migration from high-tax states support appreciation. Improvement ratio ~65%.
    • Washington (WA) — Property tax ~1.0%/yr. No state income tax (35% fed). Tech-driven demand in Seattle metro supports above-average home appreciation. Improvement ratio ~50%.
    • New York (NY) — Property tax ~1.7%/yr. Combined tax rate 50% (35% fed + 10.9% state + 3.9% NYC). Dense urban market; improvement ratio ~35% (land premium). Depreciation tax shield is most valuable here due to high marginal rate.
    • National average — Blended property tax ~1.1%/yr. Combined rate 38% (35% fed + 3% avg state). FHFA national home price index. Improvement ratio ~55%.

    关于本计算器

    本计算器免费对比美国54个地区的房地产投资与标普500、纳斯达克及60/40投资组合的历史回报，数据覆盖1970年至2025年，含2026年预测。采用FHFA房价指数与标普CoreLogic凯斯-席勒指数，模拟五种杠杆方案：全现金、35%首付、30%首付、25%首付及20%首付。租赁模式包含净营业收入（NOI）、资本化率、IRS 27.5年折旧税盾、物业管理费、HOA及空置率。支持最多三次套现再融资。售出时计算资本利得税，含25万/50万美元自住房豁免及1031交换递延。财富分解面板逐项显示每一美元回报的来源：房价升值、杠杆放大、租金收入、折旧省税及股息。

    主要发现（1995–2025年）

    1995年至2025年，加州全现金购房总回报通常低于标普500——房价年化升值约6–7%，而标普500总回报约10–11%/年（1970–2025）——原因在于低租金回报率（3–4%）且无杠杆收益。若1995年以25%首付购置橙县、尔湾或洛杉矶房产，总财富往往与标普500持平甚至超越——加州及华盛顿州海岸杠杆权益年化回报约12–15%（1995–2025）——主要受5–7%/年FHFA升值率在4倍杠杆下的复利效应及折旧税盾驱动。湾区半岛市场（阿瑟顿、帕洛阿尔托、洛斯阿尔托斯、门洛帕克）是本数据集中杠杆回报最高的地区，1995–2025年FHFA年化增长率超过7%。德克萨斯市场（达拉斯-沃斯堡、南湖、弗里斯科）租金回报率（约6%）远高于加州海岸，但升值较低（约4–5%/年），约2.1%/年的房产税是最大成本拖累。迈阿密及佛罗里达整体近年升值最为显著——凯斯-席勒年化增长率2020–2025年约10%；自1987年以来长期年均约5%——凯斯-席勒迈阿密指数2020–2025年累计涨幅超60%。西雅图和贝尔维尤1990–2025年凯斯-席勒年化增长率约6.5%；无州所得税使折旧税盾价值低于加州或纽约。曼哈顿和布鲁克林自1987年以来年均升值约5–6%——交易成本最高（约5–6%）；纽约50%综合税率使折旧税盾价值最大化。

    覆盖州份

    • 加利福尼亚州（CA） — Prop 13房产税（购买价1.25%，年涨幅上限2%）。综合税率44%（联邦35% + 州9.3%）。改良价值比例约40%。高房价租金比使全现金房产长期跑输标普500；杠杆方案因升值和折旧税盾具有竞争力。
    • 德克萨斯州（TX） — 房产税约评估价值2.1%/年。无州所得税（联邦35%）。租金回报率高于加州；改良比例约70%。就业增长推动房价升值。
    • 佛罗里达州（FL） — 自住房适用免税额；出租房税率约1.1%/年。无州所得税（联邦35%）。海岸需求及人口迁入支撑升值。改良比例约65%。
    • 华盛顿州（WA） — 房产税约1.0%/年。无州所得税（联邦35%）。西雅图都市区科技驱动需求支撑高于平均的房价升值。改良比例约50%。
    • 纽约州（NY） — 房产税约1.7%/年。综合税率50%（联邦35% + 州10.9% + 纽约市3.9%）。高房价租金比；改良比例约35%（土地溢价）。折旧税盾因边际税率高而最具价值。
    • 全国均值 — 综合房产税约1.1%/年。综合税率38%（联邦35% + 均州3%）。使用FHFA全国房价指数。改良比例约55%。
